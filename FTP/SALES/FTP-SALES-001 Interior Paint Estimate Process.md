## Purpose

Define how an FTP estimator runs an interior painting estimate, from the drive to the house through the close. The goal is consistency, professionalism, and a high close rate.

## Scope

Applies to every FTP interior painting estimate. Used by Doug and any future FTP estimators or sales hires.

## Procedure

### 1. Preparation

Before you arrive:

- Call the customer when you are on your way. Confirm the address and make sure they are expecting you.

When you arrive:

- Park on the road, not the driveway, unless the customer tells you otherwise.
- Carry a business card and a tri-fold flyer to leave behind.

At the door:

- Knock if possible. Friends knock, strangers ring the bell.
- When the customer answers, make eye contact, introduce yourself, and shake their hand firmly.
- State clearly that you are there for the interior painting estimate.

### 2. Setting the Tone

Outline the process so the customer knows what to expect:

- Gather information about the job and their motivations.
- Walk through the project together to discuss scope.
- Take measurements and document independently.
- Present the final estimate and discuss next steps.

Understand the customer's why:

- Ask open-ended questions about why they want the project done (aesthetics, repairs, upcoming event, move-in, sale).
- Listen actively and build rapport.
- Use small talk to establish a friendly, professional relationship.

### 3. Gathering the Scope

Detailed notes:

- Capture every detail needed to compose the estimate and work order.
- Document surfaces to be painted, rooms included, number of colors, and preferred sheens.

Walkthrough with the customer:

- Walk every room together.
- Flag damaged areas that need extra prep or repair.
- Note special instructions or customer preferences.

### 4. Measurements and Photos

Take measurements independently:

- After the walkthrough, measure each substrate in each room yourself. Do not rely on the customer's numbers.
- Record measurements accurately in your notes.

Photo documentation:

- Use CompanyCam to photograph every area being painted.

Set the customer's expectation:

- Tell them you will need time in their home to compile the quote.

### 5. Estimate Compilation

- Enter all measurements and project details into Paint Scout. See the Paint Scout usage SOP for field-by-field guidance.
- Build a clear, detailed, comprehensive quote.

### 6. Presenting the Estimate

Engage both decision-makers:

- Whenever possible, present to all decision-makers at the same time.

Present on a tablet with Paint Scout:

- Walk through the estimate section by section.
- Explain each line item so the customer understands what they are paying for.

Close the deal:

- Make direct eye contact and confidently state the total cost.
- Ask for the job on the spot.
- Highlight the benefit of locking in their schedule with a deposit.

Send the quote:

- Email the quote directly to the customer from Paint Scout for their records.

## Tools / Materials

- Paint Scout (tablet or phone)
- CompanyCam
- Tape measure / laser measure
- Business cards and tri-fold flyers

## Common Mistakes

1. Measuring with the customer instead of independently, which slows the visit and weakens perceived expertise.
2. Presenting the quote without both decision-makers present, then losing the sale to a "I need to talk to my spouse."
3. Leaving without asking for the deposit. The close happens on-site or it tends not to happen at all.
