## Purpose

Define exactly how PEC crew members clock in and out through BusyBusy so timecards are accurate, payroll runs clean, and travel/shop/meeting time is tracked consistently.

## Scope

Applies to all PEC field crew (installers, crew leads, helpers). Covers the daily clock-in/out cycle, shop stops, lunch breaks, meetings, and timecard review.

## Procedure

### 1. Clocking In for the Day

- Clock in when you arrive at the first job site of the day.
- If there is no shop stop planned, clock in the moment you arrive at the job site.
- If you are stopping at the shop for materials first, clock in when you leave the shop headed to the job site.

### 2. Shop Time and Material Stops

Mid-day material stop:

- Clock in as you leave the job site headed to the shop.
- While at the shop, stay clocked in under "shop time" for any task you are completing (picking up materials, loading trucks, etc.).
- When you leave the shop, clock out of "shop time" before you drive to the next job or head home.

Shop stop at the start or end of the day:

- Clock in under "shop time" as soon as you leave your last job heading to the shop.
- For morning shop stops, switch your clock to the job code as soon as you leave the shop headed to the jobsite.
- When leaving the shop at end of day, clock out before heading home.

### 3. Clocking Out for Lunch and Breaks

- Clock out before you leave for lunch or any other unpaid break.
- Lunch is capped at 30 minutes unless the crew lead or office pre-approves longer.
- Clock back in as soon as you return.
- If your lunch overlaps with travel between jobs and you are in a company truck, it is paid time. Stay clocked in.

### 4. Meetings and Non-Job Tasks

- For company meetings or training, clock in when you arrive at the shop or meeting location. Meeting time is paid.
- If a meeting or task happens after your last job, clock in when the meeting or task starts.

### 5. Early Clock-In Policy

- Do not clock in more than 15 minutes before your scheduled workday starts.
- Arriving early to load materials is fine, but it cannot be used to stretch your paid day.

### 6. Discrepancies in Timecards

- If your timecard is wrong (missed clock-in/out, wrong job code, wrong hours), report it inside BusyBusy.
- Dusty (Office Manager) reviews and corrects discrepancies during weekly payroll prep.
- You are responsible for flagging your own errors the same day you notice them.

### 7. Reviewing Timecards

- Review your own timecard at end of each day or at minimum once per week.
- Dusty reviews all timecards weekly during payroll prep and addresses errors with the crew lead.

## Tools / Materials

- BusyBusy app (installed on every crew member's phone)
- Active job codes set up by Dusty or Dylan in advance of each job

## Common Mistakes

1. Clocking in at home or while driving to the shop (not allowed).
2. Forgetting to switch job codes after a shop stop, resulting in shop time charged to a job.
3. Staying clocked in through a personal lunch.
