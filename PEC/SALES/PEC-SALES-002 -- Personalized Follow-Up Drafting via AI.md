## Purpose

Use the AI assistant (Cowork / Claude) to draft personalized text and email follow-ups for leads with pending proposals. This replaces the generic DripJobs auto-messages with messages that reference actual conversations, specific job details, and the customer's situation, which should improve response rates and close rates.

---

## Scope

**Who this applies to:** Dylan
**When to use this SOP:** Any time you are resending or following up on a pending proposal and want a message that sounds like you wrote it, not a template bot.

---

## Prerequisites

Before starting, make sure:
- [ ] The lead has a job/proposal in DripJobs
- [ ] At least one conversation has happened (call, text, or in-person estimate) so there is context to personalize against
- [ ] You know what action you want the customer to take (approve proposal, pick a color, schedule, call back, etc.)

---

## Steps

1. **Open the PEC Sales Engine project in Cowork** and tell the assistant which customer you need a follow-up for. Include what you want to send (text, email, or both) and any context the assistant does not already have (e.g., "I just talked to him about a grind and stain option, resending the updated quote now").

2. **The assistant pulls the customer record from DripJobs via Chrome.** It will gather:
   - Contact info (name, phone, email, address)
   - Proposal details (line items, pricing, optional items, total)
   - Job notes (crew notes, client notes, company notes)
   - Activity log (proposal sent/viewed dates, stage changes)
   - Chat/SMS history (all text messages sent and received)
   - QUO tab call log and transcripts (every phone call with timestamps and full transcripts)

3. **The assistant synthesizes the data** and identifies:
   - What was discussed on the most recent call(s)
   - What the customer's objections or questions were
   - Whether the customer is comparing other quotes
   - What the logical next step is for the customer
   - Any personal details that make the message feel human (job, schedule constraints, neighborhood, referral source)

4. **The assistant drafts two messages:**
   - **Text message:** Short, conversational, references the last conversation, includes a clear call to action. Should sound like Dylan typed it on his phone.
   - **Email:** Slightly more detailed. Recaps what was discussed, lays out what is included in the price, states the next step clearly, mentions financing if relevant. Professional but not stiff.

5. **Dylan reviews and edits.** Read both drafts. Adjust tone, add anything the assistant missed, remove anything that feels off. The assistant does not send anything without your approval.

6. **Send via DripJobs.** Use the DripJobs Chat to send the text and the DripJobs email function (or direct email) to send the email. Resend the proposal using the "Re-Send Proposal" button on the job page.

---

## Decision Points

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| No call transcripts available (text/email only) | Assistant drafts based on proposal details, notes, and SMS history. Messages will be less personalized but still better than templates. |
| Customer has not viewed proposal at all | Lead with value prop and curiosity. Do not assume they know pricing. |
| Customer viewed proposal but no response | Acknowledge you saw interest, ask if they have questions, restate next step. |
| Customer is comparing multiple quotes | Emphasize PEC differentiators: 10-year warranty, dedicated crew, showroom, financing. Do not trash competitors. |
| Customer mentioned budget concerns | Lead with the most affordable option, mention 0% financing through Enhancify. |
| Customer has been unresponsive 7+ days | Shift tone to "checking in" / "no pressure" rather than pushing. |

---

## Escalation

Escalate to Dylan when:
- The assistant cannot access DripJobs or the QUO tab is not loading
- A customer has responded negatively or requested no further contact
- The assistant is unsure about pricing, scope, or what was promised on a call

---

## Quality Check

Before sending, confirm:
- [ ] The message references something specific from your actual conversation (not generic)
- [ ] Pricing matches the current proposal in DripJobs
- [ ] The next step / call to action is clear and simple
- [ ] Tone sounds like Dylan, not a bot
- [ ] No information is fabricated (if unsure about a detail from a call, leave it out)
- [ ] Financing is mentioned only if relevant to the customer's situation
