## Purpose

Ensure all scope changes, surprises, and additional work are priced, communicated, and approved in writing before the crew touches them. Protects margin, the schedule, and the customer relationship.

## Scope

Applies to every PEC job where work falls outside the signed original estimate. Applies to every crew lead, installer, and office team member involved in PEC jobs.

## Procedure

### 1. When a Change Order Is Required

A change order (CO) is required any time work falls outside the original estimate or adds labor, materials, or time. Examples:

- Damage caused by others (paint, drywall mud, oil, glue, stains, homeowner-created issues).
- Concrete conditions uncovered after grinding: deep cracks, delamination, soft or crumbly substrate, large patching areas.
- Extra services added by the customer: additional room, stem wall, stairs, closets, expanded prep, extra topcoat.
- Anything requiring additional hours, product, or a modified workflow.

If it is extra, it needs a change order. No exceptions.

### 2. Chain of Command

Installer to Crew Lead to Dylan to Customer (via Crew Lead).

- Installers do not talk to customers about scope changes.
- Crew leads identify the issue, document it, and notify Dylan.
- Dylan approves pricing and the plan.
- Crew lead communicates the CO to the customer using Dylan's direction.
- Dylan creates and sends the official CO through DripJobs.
- Dusty (Office Manager) is informed only if the CO affects scheduling.

### 3. Documentation the Crew Lead Must Submit

Before Dylan prices the CO, the crew lead sends:

- CompanyCam photos of the issue.
- Short written description: what changed, why it changed, what work is needed.
- Notes on materials and estimated added time.

Dylan reviews and determines pricing.

### 4. Pricing Rules

Pricing is a mix of flat rates and time-and-material:

- Flat rates for predictable add-ons (common room adds, closets, stairs, stem walls, standard patch work).
- Time-and-material for unknowns (large delamination, heavy patch work, unexpected substrate failures).
- Dylan decides which method applies for each scenario.
- No minimum charge. Pricing matches the work required.

### 5. Customer Communication

Who speaks to the customer:

- Crew lead only. Installers do not explain or negotiate change orders.

How communication works:

- Dylan sets price and wording.
- Crew lead explains the situation on-site in simple language.
- Crew lead tells the customer to expect a formal CO in DripJobs from Dylan.
- Dylan sends the digital CO for signature.
- No work continues until the CO is signed in DripJobs.
- If the customer declines the CO, work stops on that item and the lead follows Dylan's direction.

### 6. Creation and Approval

Dylan (Owner / Sales):

- Reviews the issue.
- Determines scope and pricing.
- Creates the formal CO in DripJobs.
- Sends the CO to the customer for signature.
- Updates CRM notes.

Dusty (Office Manager):

- Informed only if the CO affects scheduling.
- Adjusts the schedule if needed.
- No field decision-making.

### 7. Work Freeze Rule

No change order work begins until the signed CO is completed in DripJobs. Zero exceptions.

### 8. After Approval

Once the CO is signed:

- Crew lead proceeds with the work.
- Installers follow the new direction.
- Dylan updates job notes.
- Dusty adjusts the schedule if duration changes.
- Job continues as normal.

### 9. Enforcement

Breaking CO protocol causes financial loss, customer confusion, unpaid labor, and inconsistent execution.

- Any crew member bypassing this SOP is written up and retrained.
- Any lead bypassing this SOP meets with Dylan for corrective action.

## Tools / Materials

- DripJobs CRM (for creating and sending the CO)
- CompanyCam (for photo documentation)

## Common Mistakes

1. Installer discusses pricing or scope directly with the customer.
2. Crew continues working before the CO is signed in DripJobs.
3. Crew lead verbally agrees to a price without looping Dylan in first.
